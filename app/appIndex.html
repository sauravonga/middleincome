MIDDLE INCOME

The World Bank determines the threshold for classification of countries by Gross National Income per capita. New thresholds are determined at the start of the Bank’s fiscal year in July and remain fixed for twelve months regardless of subsequent revisions to estimates.

Low Income
       below $995
Lower Middle Income
       $996 to $3,895
Upper Middle Income
       $3,896 to $12,055
High Income
       above $12,055

TRAPS & TRANSITIONS

Some economies are trapped into perpetual cycle of stagnant
growth while some are able to transition to high income

TRAPS

An economy squeezed between the low-wage poor-country competitors that dominate in mature industries and the rich-country innovators that dominate in industries undergoing rapid technological change.

TRANSITIONS

Successfully moving an economy from middle income to rich, under proper circumstances and policies by balancing manufacturing competitiveness and fostering a culture of technological innovation.

INDICATORS OF TRANSITIONS

A multi-pronged approach to policy making ensures that an economy is able to transition to high income.
